Exploring the Best Tourist Places to Stay in Rainy Season Abroad

10 July

As the rainy season sets in across different parts of the world, it brings with it a unique charm that transforms landscapes and experiences. Traveling during the rainy season can be a delight for those who enjoy lush greenery, refreshing showers, and a quieter, more serene atmosphere in popular tourist destinations. Whether you're seeking a romantic getaway, a rejuvenating solo trip, or a family adventure, choosing the right place to stay can significantly enhance your rainy season travel experience. Here's a curated list of some of the

best tourist places to stay abroad during the rainy season

.


1. Ubud, Bali, Indonesia


Bali, often dubbed as the "Island of the Gods," becomes especially enchanting during the rainy season (usually from November to March). Ubud, nestled in the heart of Bali's lush countryside, is a haven for travelers seeking tranquility and cultural immersion. Surrounded by rice paddies, rainforests, and ancient temples, Ubud offers a range of accommodation options from luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. The rainy season here brings occasional downpours interspersed with sunny intervals, making it ideal for exploring without the crowds. Don't miss the Ubud Monkey Forest, Tegallalang Rice Terraces, and the vibrant Ubud Art Market during your stay.



2. Tokyo, Japan


Japan's capital city, Tokyo, experiences its rainy season, known as "tsuyu" or the plum rain, typically from June to mid-July. While the showers can be heavy, they bring out the city's vibrant colors and offer a different perspective of its bustling streets and serene parks. Stay in Tokyo to experience its eclectic mix of traditional and modern attractions, including the historic Asakusa district, the serene Meiji Shrine, and the futuristic Shibuya Crossing. Tokyo offers a plethora of accommodation choices ranging from capsule hotels to luxury skyscraper hotels with stunning city views.



3. Amsterdam, Netherlands


Amsterdam, famous for its canals, tulips, and cultural richness, is a delightful destination to visit during the rainy season (October to March). The cityscape, adorned with historic buildings and crisscrossed by picturesque waterways, takes on a romantic charm in the rain. Explore world-class museums such as the Rijksmuseum and the Van Gogh Museum, take a leisurely boat cruise along the canals, or simply wander through the charming neighborhoods like Jordaan and De Pijp. Accommodation options in Amsterdam range from cozy canal-side apartments to boutique hotels in historic buildings.



4. Queenstown, New Zealand

Queenstown, nestled on New Zealand's South Island, is a year-round destination known for its stunning landscapes and outdoor adventures. During the rainy season (autumn and winter months), the surrounding mountains and lakes become even more dramatic, shrouded in mist and occasional rain showers. Stay in Queenstown to explore nearby attractions such as Milford Sound, Fiordland National Park, and the scenic Glenorchy. Choose from luxury lodges overlooking Lake Wakatipu to charming bed and breakfasts in the town center for a cozy retreat after a day of exploring.



5. Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Rio de Janeiro, with its vibrant culture, stunning beaches, and lush rainforests, is a captivating destination to visit during its rainy season (December to March). While showers are common, they often clear up quickly, leaving behind fresh air and clearer skies. Stay in Rio to experience the iconic Copacabana and Ipanema beaches, visit the towering Christ the Redeemer statue atop Corcovado Mountain, and explore the bohemian neighborhood of Santa Teresa. Choose from beachfront hotels with stunning ocean views to boutique guesthouses tucked away in Rio's hills for a memorable stay.



6. Cape Town, South Africa


Cape Town, located on the southwestern tip of South Africa, experiences its rainy season during the winter months (June to August). The city, surrounded by the majestic Table Mountain and pristine beaches, offers a mix of cultural experiences and natural beauty. Stay in Cape Town to explore the vibrant Victoria & Alfred Waterfront, visit the historic Robben Island, and take a scenic drive along the Cape Peninsula. Accommodation options range from luxury hotels in the city center to charming guest houses in the nearby wine regions of Stellenbosch and Franschhoek.



7. Portland, Oregon, USA


Portland, known for its hipster culture, craft breweries, and lush greenery, experiences a rainy season from late fall to early spring. The frequent rain showers contribute to Portland's reputation as a haven for coffee lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. Stay in Portland to explore its eclectic neighborhoods such as Pearl District and Alberta Arts District, visit the world-famous International Rose Test Garden, and take a day trip to the stunning Columbia River Gorge. Choose from boutique hotels in downtown Portland to cozy bed and breakfasts in the city's leafy suburbs.



8. Lisbon, Portugal


Lisbon, Portugal's vibrant capital city, enjoys a mild climate with occasional showers during the winter months (November to March). The city's charming cobbled streets, historic monuments, and panoramic views of the Tagus River make it a delightful destination year-round. Stay in Lisbon to explore the historic district of Alfama, visit the iconic Belém Tower and Jerónimos Monastery, and indulge in Portugal's culinary delights at local eateries and markets. Accommodation options in Lisbon range from boutique hotels in renovated palaces to stylish apartments in the trendy neighborhoods of Bairro Alto and Chiado.



9. Kerala, India


Kerala, located in southern India, experiences its monsoon season from June to September, transforming the region into a lush green paradise. Known as "God's Own Country," Kerala offers serene backwaters, tranquil beaches, and Ayurvedic retreats. Stay in Kerala to experience a houseboat cruise on the backwaters of Alleppey, explore the tea plantations of Munnar, and unwind on the pristine beaches of Kovalam. Accommodation options in Kerala range from luxurious resorts overlooking the Arabian Sea to eco-friendly retreats nestled in the Western Ghats.



10. Costa Rica


Costa Rica, known for its biodiversity and eco-tourism opportunities, experiences a rainy season from May to November, referred to as the "green season." The rain showers rejuvenate the lush rainforests, making it an ideal time for wildlife spotting and outdoor adventures. Stay in Costa Rica to explore the Monteverde Cloud Forest Reserve, visit the Arenal Volcano National Park, and relax on the pristine beaches of Manuel Antonio. Accommodation options in Costa Rica include eco-lodges nestled in the rainforest, all-inclusive resorts along the Pacific coast, and cozy cabins in the mountains.
