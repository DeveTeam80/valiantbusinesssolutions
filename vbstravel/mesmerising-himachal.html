Mesmerising Himachal

One can enjoy the best of adventure, honeymooning, vacationing, and more in Himachal Pradesh, one of India's most sought-after travel destinations, with a

Himachal Pradesh tour package from Pune.

Thanks to its breathtaking landscapes, verdant meadows, abundant wildlife, uncharted territories, distinct cultural legacy, tumultuous bazaars, and a plethora of adventure activities. Colonial architecture adorns the landscapes of Shimla and Dalhousie, while enchanting shrines and temples dot the Kangra valley. Additionally, Dharamshala offers a deeper sense of spirituality, while the mountains of Kullu and Manali beckon adventure seekers. Himachal is traversed by the Shivalik, Dhauladhar, Pir Panjal, Great Himalayan, and Zanskar ranges of the Himalayan ranges. The elevations of these mountains range from 460 to 6000 meters. The huge state's terrain ranges from verdant subtropical valleys to chilly, snow-capped alpine mountains...

Adventure in Himachal Pradesh

Himachal’s sheer geographical diversity makes it one of the few places in the world blessed with natural riches. The winding rivers through deep gorges, calm mountain lakes, snow-peaked mountains, and lush forests, all make great venues for adventure.

River Rafting:

The rivers that originate at the Himalayan glacial valleys in Himachal Pradesh descend rapidly. Indian and international white water rafters have also been drawn to these courses. There are guided rafting options available on the River Sutlej near Tattapani, the River Ravi near Chamba, the Pirdi on the River Beas near Kullu, and the Spiti River in Lahaul and the Spiti district for the more adventurous. During your Himachal tour, white water rafting would be one of the most exhilarating activities.

Paragliding:

Maybe paragliding is the closest thing you can get to sailing the skies like a free bird. The best places in India and the world to go paragliding are in Himachal Pradesh. In the Kangra district, at Bir Billing, you can go tandem or solo paragliding. International competitions in the sport, including the Paragliding World Cup, are regularly held in the region. With their gentle winds and rolling hills, Billing's hills are fantastic for takeoffs, and Bir's plains offer fantastic terraced landings. Solang Valley, which is located 14 kilometers northwest of Manali town, is another well-liked paragliding location in the state.

Trekking:

Trekking is even more thrilling in the Himalayan mountains, which encompass the entirety of Himachal Pradesh. Countless paths lead past orchards and paddy fields, climb over mountain peaks, pass through dense forests, cross swift-moving streams and waterfalls, pass beneath oak or rhododendron trees in full bloom, lead to glacial lakes, and wind up to mountaintop pilgrimage routes. It makes sense—Himachal is a hiking haven for enthusiasts.

Skiing:

One of the few locations in India where skiing is possible is Himachal Pradesh. At the Shimla district's Narkanda ski resort, Himachal Tourism provides ski lessons and equipment rentals. In addition, professional and amateur skiing lessons are held on the slopes of Solang Nala, Rohtang Pass, and Hanuman Tibba in Manali. You can try heli-skiing to improve your skiing experience. You are airlifted from Manali to high-altitude snow slopes where you embark on an exhilarating Heli-skiing adventure.

When to Visit Himachal Pradesh



Summer: Himachal Pradesh, one of India's most popular summer travel destinations, is a delight to visit any month of the year. For those who enjoy the fresh mountain air and mountains, its healthy climate is the perfect getaway. The summer season, which runs from March to June, sees a massive influx of both domestic and foreign tourists. The summer months are ideal for travel to Himachal Pradesh because the region becomes hospitable and pleasant. The range of temperatures is 22–37 degrees Celsius.

Monsoon: Although the July–September monsoons are incredibly beautiful, they are also dangerous due to landslides. Since it's off-peak, lodging is more affordable and easily accessible. Right now, the only drawback is that trekking, hiking, and other adventure opportunities have been closed. It's between eighteen and thirty degrees Celsius at this time of year.
