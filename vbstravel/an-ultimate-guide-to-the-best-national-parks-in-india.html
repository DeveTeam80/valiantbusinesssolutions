An Ultimate Guide to The Best National Parks in India

25 August

Since ancient times, Indian wildlife has been the country's main attraction for tourists. About 400 different species of animals have natural habitats in India thanks to its varied terrain and climatic conditions. India's flora and fauna are defined by uncommon and endangered species thanks to the country's abundance and diversity.

India, a land of diverse landscapes and abundant wildlife, is home to some of the most breathtaking national parks that offer a glimpse into the country's rich biodiversity. From the majestic tigers prowling through dense jungles to the graceful elephants wandering across lush grasslands, each national park is a testament to India's natural wonders. Embark on a journey with us as we explore the crown jewels of India's wildlife conservation efforts, inviting you to connect with nature in ways you've never imagined.

Discovering India's Wildlife Treasures:


1. Ranthambore National Park :

The massive Ranthambore fort, standing 700 feet tall, is located inside the park area. Ancient Jain and Hindu temples can also be visited. Near Padma Talao, the park's main lake, there is a banyan tree that is reputed to be the second-largest in India. Watch out for tigers, as well as leopards, wild boars, hyenas, spotted deer, and many other animals.

Place : Rajasthan
Activities : Gypsy Safari, Kachida Valley, and the reserve's on-site Ranthambore Fort are all recommended.

2. Jim Corbett National Park:

The first of its kind in India, Jim Corbett National Park in Uttarakhand is a realm of raw wilderness. Named after the legendary hunter-turned-conservationist, this park offers a chance to spot elusive tigers, leopards, and vibrant birdlife. Embark on an enthralling safari through its diverse landscapes and let the echoes of the jungle captivate your senses.

Place : Uttarakhand
Activities : Partake in exhilarating jeep safaris amid the rugged terrain of Jim Corbett National Park, seeking encounters with its iconic Bengal tigers and diverse wildlife.







3. Kaziranga National Park :

A symphony of grasslands, wetlands, and mighty rivers, Kaziranga is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a stronghold of the one-horned rhinoceros. As you navigate its floodplains, keep your binoculars ready to witness a colorful parade of birds and the occasional glimpse of a stealthy tiger.

Place : Assam
Activities : Jeep safaris, elephant safaris, and wildlife viewing.




4. Periyar National Park :

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Periyar National Park in Kerala offers a unique boat safari experience on its placid lake. The mist-kissed hills serve as a dramatic setting for encounters with herds of elephants, langurs, and the occasional leopard that grace its shores.

Place : Kerala
Activities : Explore Periyar National Park through boat safaris on its tranquil lake, providing opportunities for wildlife spotting amidst lush surroundings.




5. Gir Wildlife Sanctuary :

The last bastion of the Asiatic lion, Gir Wildlife Sanctuary in Gujarat celebrates the success of conservation efforts. Traverse through its rugged terrain to witness the regal big cats and engage with a vibrant ecosystem of diverse fauna, including leopards, hyenas, and birds.

Place : Gujarat
Activities : Jeep safari with a view of the wild Asiatic Lions.




6. Kanha National Park :

The enchanting landscapes of Kanha, which inspired "The Jungle Book," are a realm of enchantment. Rolling meadows and dense sal forests set the stage for your encounter with the rare Barasingha deer, playful langurs, and the mystical royal Bengal tiger.

Place : Madhya Pradesh
Activities : safaris with elephants, jeeps, and wildlife viewing, etc.




7. Sundarbans National Park :

In the mystical mangrove forests of the Sundarbans, where land and water intertwine, adventure awaits. Navigating through its winding channels, you'll witness the intriguing adaptations of the Bengal tiger to its watery home, along with estuarine crocodiles and a myriad of bird species.

Place : West Bengal
Activities : Tiger sightings, bird gazing, turtle walks, and saltwater crocodile viewing.




8. Mudumalai National Park:

Nestled in the Nilgiri Hills, Mudumalai is a haven for nature enthusiasts. Its rich biodiversity includes elephants, gaurs, and elusive predators like leopards and dholes. Traverse its dense thickets and grassy meadows for a chance to glimpse these captivating creatures.

Place : Karnataka
Activities : Engage in wildlife safaris, bird watching, nature walks, elephant interactions, bamboo rafting, photography, camping, and more in Mudumalai National Park




India's national parks offer a glimpse into the raw beauty and diversity of the natural world. Each park is a testament to the country's commitment to preserving its unique flora and fauna.
